Page 1

Final Topic

Problem

When you’re out of town or new to town, it’s hard to find a good, reliable barber/hair stylist. College students have to search through Google Maps and Yelp to get ratings, price, and locations - and even then you don’t know if those people have similar preferences as you do.

Solution

A web application that pools all barbers/hair stylists in an area and shows them on a map for convenience. The user can see the cost, set up an appointment, and get directions. There would be an option open up the location in maps or Uber/Lyft to the barbershop. Ratings would be crowdsourced, and people can upload photos of their cuts from that location - and an Instagram-type feed with photos and links to the barbers. Ratings could be sorted by distance, price, or based on the profiles of the reviewers, like their age. There would be a dashboard for the user (with login) to see their appointment time, past barbers, and transaction history.

Page 2

Different Modes:

Guest mode:

  • Guests can look at reviews of shops in the area
  • They can look at images that people have submitted
  • They cannot bookmark/save any places until they make an account and save that in their profile
  • Certain features won’t be visible because they’re in guest mode like saving salons, etc

    • User mode:

  • Can see posts/reviews made by other people
  • Can save hair salons
  • Choose from available times that the stylists have and make an appointment there
  • Stylist can accept or decline the offer
  • View History

    • Business mode:

  • Stylists can set times that they are available
  • They can post and view other content
  • They can manually add appointments to the calendar

    • Admin mode:

  • Supervises events
  • Takes complaints
